Immersive Commodity Telepresence with the AVATRINA Robot Avatar

The project entails the design, development, and validation of a teleoperated robot system called AVATRINA. This system aims to replicate human presence and interaction capabilities remotely, targeting applications such as telemedicine, disaster response, and complex industrial tasks. It integrates advanced robotics, real-time control, and immersive telepresence technologies to allow operators to perform tasks in distant environments as if they were physically present.

Technical development of AVATRINA involved the construction of a humanoid robot with articulated limbs and dexterous hands, capable of mimicking the operator's movements with high fidelity. The system employed motion capture technology to track the operator's gestures and movements, translating them into robot actions. Real-time feedback was facilitated through a VR (Virtual Reality) headset, providing the operator with a 3D stereoscopic view from the robot’s perspective, and haptic gloves to deliver tactile feedback. The robot was equipped with advanced sensors, including depth cameras and force sensors, to enhance environmental interaction and manipulation tasks. Software integration was accomplished using ROS (Robot Operating System) for modular control and data fusion.

In the ANA Avatar XPRIZE competition, AVATRINA demonstrated its capabilities in executing complex, multi-step tasks that required precise manipulation, agile navigation, and interactive communication. The system successfully completed tasks such as navigating through debris-filled environments, manipulating various tools, and performing delicate assembly tasks. Technical evaluations highlighted the system’s ability to provide immersive sensory feedback, precise control, and robust performance under latency constraints, leading to its 4th place ranking in the competition.

The project's outcomes illustrate the feasibility and efficiency of using teleoperated robotic avatars for complex human task replication in remote settings. The technical achievements of AVATRINA, particularly in terms of its sensory integration, motion fidelity, and real-time feedback, set a benchmark for future developments in the field. Challenges such as latency management, operator cognitive load, and system reliability were identified as critical areas for future research. Enhancements in AI-driven autonomy and improved ergonomic interfaces are seen as essential for the next generation of immersive telepresence systems.
